## About

Window and door installation is unforgiving work. A measurement that's off by a centimeter means a return visit, a delayed job, a frustrated client. The professionals who do this work don't have room for tools that let them down — especially not on a job site with no reliable signal.

Medida had already built something real. Their iOS app used ARKit to let installers capture precise measurements on-site, generate reports, and move on. The core idea worked. But as the product reached more professional installers in the field, the gaps became harder to ignore. AR rendering had room to improve. The app's behavior under poor connectivity was unpredictable. High-res measurement files could be lost if the app exited at the wrong moment. For a consumer app, these would be rough edges. For a tool that professionals stake their work on, they were blockers.

They brought in Applift not to rebuild, but to harden. The brief was to take a working product and make it professional-grade — the kind of tool installers reach for first because it's never let them down.

That meant going deep on three fronts: tightening the AR measurement pipeline for accuracy and speed, building a background upload system that could survive device locks and dropped connections, and improving overall performance to the point where the app felt as solid as the work it supported.

## The Outcome

The app that came out of the engagement was materially different from the one that went in — not in what it did, but in how reliably it did it. AR measurements that used to require multiple attempts now land cleanly. Files that used to depend on the installer staying in the app long enough to upload now move in the background, invisibly, regardless of what the device does next.

For professionals, that distinction matters. A tool that works most of the time isn't a professional tool. Medida now has a product built to the same standard as the work it supports — one that installers can trust on every job, in every condition.
