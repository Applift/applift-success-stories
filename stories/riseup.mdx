## Bridging the Mobile Gap
RiseUp had already established a trusted platform for financial wellness, but user engagement was shifting decisively toward mobile. They required more than just external developers; they needed a strategic engineering partner to establish an architectural foundation capable of supporting rapid feature growth and international expansion.

After analyzing the long-term roadmap, Applift and RiseUp committed to a fully native approach. This decision prioritized performance, platform-specific fidelity, and a robust codebase that would remain maintainable as the product matured.

## Building the Platform and the Team
Applift integrated a dedicated squad of senior mobile engineers directly into the RiseUp organization, operating as an extension of their product team. While executing the native iOS and Android builds, we led the technical recruitment and onboarding of RiseUp’s internal mobile group. By sharing architectural standards and conducting real-time code reviews, we ensured that the team and the platform matured in lockstep.

When RiseUp expanded into the Israeli market, the partnership evolved to meet the new requirements. Collaborating with RiseUp’s newly established internal team, we co-developed a Flutter application for the regional launch, including the implementation of localized in-app purchase flows.

## A Mobile Practice Built to Keep Growing

The native transition provided UK users with a mobile experience that matched the maturity of RiseUp's web platform. Simultaneously, the Israeli launch addressed a critical market need through tailored in-app purchase flows.

The engagement concluded with a successful handoff: RiseUp now operates a mature, self-sufficient mobile division that owns a codebase built on professional standards. With users reporting an average monthly cash flow increase of $340, the project demonstrates that high-performance engineering is a direct driver of financial outcomes.
