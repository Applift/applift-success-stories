<ExecutiveSummary>
Jotit had a mature Android product and a closing window to reach a rapidly iPad-first school market. Applift helped them build a **native iPad app** with the architectural foundations to last for years — not a rushed port.
</ExecutiveSummary>

<div style={{ fontSize: '1rem', lineHeight: 1.8, color: 'var(--ss-text-secondary)' }}>

Jotit had already done something hard: it built a meaningful education product in a space where most software still forces students to adapt to rigid digital systems. The team had spent years shaping an experience that felt closer to how students actually learn — through writing, annotating, organizing, and engaging with school material in a natural way. They had a strong Android product, a clear vision, and real traction.

But the market was moving. More school districts were adopting iPads, and that shift created a gap Jotit could no longer ignore. Without an iOS product, important opportunities would remain out of reach. This wasn't just a platform expansion — it was a strategic move the company needed to make, and it needed to make it fast.

That was the moment they approached Applift. Jotit wasn't looking for extra hands to push tickets forward. They were looking for a partner who could help them make the right product and architectural decisions early, move quickly under real time pressure, and build something strong enough to support the company for years to come.

</div>

<div style={{ fontSize: '1rem', lineHeight: 1.8, color: 'var(--ss-text-secondary)', marginTop: '3rem' }}>

Once the collaboration began, the relationship quickly became more than a standard client-vendor setup.

Jotit came in with a strong product vision and deep understanding of its users, but without dedicated iOS expertise in-house. Applift brought the missing layer: native iOS experience, architectural thinking, and the ability to translate a mature product into a new platform without reducing it to a rushed copy of what already existed.

That mattered early. The first stage of the project wasn't just about starting development — it was about setting direction. Jotit needed confidence that the product would be built natively, thoughtfully, and in a way that wouldn't create technical debt they would regret later. Applift helped shape that foundation from the beginning, guiding key decisions around approach, structure, and tradeoffs.

From there, the work became highly collaborative. Product discussions, sprint planning, delivery tradeoffs, and technical decisions were handled in an ongoing rhythm. Applift wasn't simply executing a spec — the team was involved in helping Jotit think through what needed to happen now, what could wait, and what had to be done properly from the start.

As the project progressed, the product itself took shape in layers. First came the iOS foundation: the core structure needed to support a native iPad experience. Then came the product work that would make that foundation meaningful — the flows, interactions, and interface decisions that turned the app into something usable and polished. Over time, the result became more than an iOS version of an existing product. It became a real extension of Jotit's platform, built for the expectations and interaction patterns of iPad users.

</div>

<div style={{ fontSize: '1rem', lineHeight: 1.8, color: 'var(--ss-text-secondary)', marginTop: '3rem' }}>

For Jotit, this project wasn't just about launching on another platform. It was about removing a strategic limitation at exactly the right moment. The company already had a strong product and a clear point of view. What it needed was a way to carry that strength into a market that was increasingly moving toward iPads. With Applift, Jotit was able to do that in a way that didn't sacrifice quality for speed.

The result was bigger than the app itself. Jotit gained access to new opportunities, strengthened its product foundation, and moved from having a strong single-platform presence to having a broader, more durable position in the market. What started as an urgent delivery need became a long-term product and business advantage.
