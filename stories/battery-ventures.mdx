Battery is a global, technology-focused investment firm. They needed an in-house tool designed to serve their VC investment team around the globe. The project involves an enormous number of complex full-stack features and integrations with many third-party SDKs and services — requiring a team that could handle the breadth and depth of a sophisticated internal product.
</Challenge>

<Partnership>
Building an internal tool for a global VC firm requires understanding workflows that are highly specific and constantly evolving. Applift's team embedded deeply into Battery's product process, learning the nuances of how investment professionals work across time zones, data sources, and decision cycles.

The breadth of the project — spanning a React frontend, Node.js and Python backends, and a large number of third-party integrations — demanded a team comfortable with ambiguity and context-switching. Applift delivered iteratively, shipping new capabilities continuously while keeping the platform stable for a team that depends on it daily.
</Partnership>
