## About

OpenWeb's goal is to improve online conversation. They work with publishers and brands to grow direct audience relationships through comment sections and community features.

More than 1,000 publishers meant the technical problem was genuinely hard. Each one with its own tech stack, brand requirements, integration preferences. They needed SDKs that could absorb that diversity without turning every deployment into a bespoke project. A major news network and a niche vertical publisher had to both get what they needed, without compromise.

All three platforms needed to ship in parallel: iOS, Android, and React Native.

Applift's engineers joined OpenWeb's mobile group and worked across all three SDKs at once. Shared review practices and consistent standards kept the work coherent even as it spread across separate codebases. Speed mattered, but not at the cost of the quality bar that 1,000+ publishers were depending on.

## The Outcome

Three production SDKs, all running in parallel: native iOS, native Android, and React Native. Each with a customizable UI system publishers can tailor to their brand, and Google Ads integration included.

The architecture handles real diversity. Publishers come in with wildly different stacks and requirements, and the SDKs absorb that without forcing a lowest-common-denominator experience. That's what let the platform scale to where it is.
