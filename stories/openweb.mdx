## About

OpenWeb's goal is to improve online conversation. They work with publishers and brands to grow direct audience relationships through comment sections and community features.

More than 1,000 publishers meant the technical problem was genuinely hard. Each one with its own tech stack, brand requirements, integration preferences. The SDKs had to absorb that diversity without turning every deployment into a bespoke project. A major news network and a niche vertical publisher both had to get what they needed, without compromise.

## Three Platforms, One Standard

[iOS](https://developers.openweb.com/docs/ios-getting-started) and [Android](https://developers.openweb.com/docs/android-social-sdk-getting-started) came first; [React Native](https://developers.openweb.com/docs/rn-getting-started) joined the scope in late 2025. Applift's engineers are embedded in OpenWeb's mobile group and maintain all three SDKs. Shared review practices and consistent standards keep the work coherent across separate codebases.

## The Outcome

Three production SDKs shipped: native iOS, native Android, and React Native. Each maintained from one shared standard, not splintered into bespoke builds per publisher - which is what let the platform scale to where it is.
